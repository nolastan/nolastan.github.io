Stanford Rosenthal is a Senior Product Designer based in San Francisco and doing UX work at Laude, a nonprofit that convenes, resources, and amplifies computer scientists that ship their research and impact the lives of billions.

I've been designing and building for the web for 17 years–it‘s what I love to do.

I grew up in New Orleans. At 15, after Hurricane Katrina exposed the failures of the levee system, my mother and I co-founded Levees.org to push back on the myths surrounding what happened. That experience — making something real out of outrage and a domain name — shaped how I think about design and technology to this day. (The full story is told in an award-winning book, Words Whispered in Water.)

My career has been deliberately non-linear. I left college to join a startup, and have been an early team member at several others. I built a UX design curriculum that ranked #1 on Course Report and led a team of 45 developing and delivering software engineering curriculum. I‘ve designed products that help people save money, find work, review medical bills, and reduce paper waste.

What ties it together isn‘t a category or an industry — it‘s a question I keep asking: how do we design systems that make people‘s lives genuinely better, especially people who aren‘t usually centered in product conversations?

Right now that question is pulling me toward climate adaptation and democratic AI.

When I'm not designing or coding, I'm usually prepping produce from the farmer's market, trying to grow some myself, and finding excuses to share it with others.
