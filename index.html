Stanford Rosenthal is a Senior Product Designer currently doing UX work at Laude, a non-profit AI research institute. He lives in San Francisco, where he's also turning a duplex near the Castro into a community gathering space.

About

I've been designing and building for the web for 17 years, and somehow it still doesn't feel like work.

I grew up in New Orleans. At 15, after Hurricane Katrina exposed the failures of the levee system, my mother and I co-founded Levees.org to push back on the official story of what happened. That experience — making something real out of outrage and a domain name — shaped how I think about design and technology to this day. (The full story is told in an award-winning book, Words Whispered in Water.)

My career has been deliberately non-linear. I left college to join a startup. I left a cushy job at LinkedIn and took a 60% pay cut to work on something I believed in. I built a UX design curriculum that ranked #1 on Course Report and led a team of 45 to deliver it. I've designed products that help people save money, find work, review medical bills, and reduce paper waste.

What ties it together isn't a category or an industry — it's a question I keep asking: how do we design systems that make people's lives genuinely better, especially people who aren't usually centered in product conversations?

Right now that question is pulling me toward climate, AI, and the unglamorous problems that don't have obvious business models yet.

When I'm not designing or coding, I'm usually cooking vegetarian meals from local ingredients, trying to grow some of them myself, or finding excuses to host people at home.
